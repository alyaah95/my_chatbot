Geographical Features

The Nile River

The world's longest river, flowing over 6,600 km from central Africa to the Mediterranean. Ancient Egypt was called "the gift of the Nile" as its annual flooding created fertile land.

Western Desert

Part of the Sahara, covering about two-thirds of Egypt's land area. Includes several oases like Siwa, Bahariya, Farafra, Dakhla, and Kharga.

Red Sea Coast

Famous for its coral reefs and marine life, the Red Sea coast is a popular destination for diving and snorkeling, with resort cities like Hurghada and Sharm El Sheikh.
